# Refract.trade - Regulatory Compliance Framework

## Regulatory Landscape Overview

### Primary Regulatory Bodies
- **SEC**: Securities and Exchange Commission (investment adviser registration)
- **FINRA**: Financial Industry Regulatory Authority (broker-dealer oversight)
- **CFTC**: Commodity Futures Trading Commission (futures and options)
- **State Regulators**: State securities divisions (varies by state)
- **International**: FCA (UK), ESMA (EU) for future expansion

## Business Model Regulatory Classification

### Current Approach: Technology Platform + Advisory
**Classification**: Investment Adviser (not broker-dealer)
**Registration**: SEC Investment Adviser (Form ADV)
**Rationale**: Providing automated investment advice requires IA registration

### Alternative Approach: Pure Technology Platform
**Classification**: Technology service provider
**Registration**: State business license only
**Requirements**: Strict disclaimers, no specific investment advice
**Limitations**: Cannot provide personalized recommendations

## Required Registrations & Licenses

### SEC Investment Adviser Registration
```
Form ADV Filing Requirements:
- Part 1: Business operations, services, fees
- Part 2: Disclosure brochure for clients
- Part 3: Form CRS (Customer Relationship Summary)

Requirements:
- Assets Under Management: >$100M (federal threshold)
- Compliance Officer: Designated individual
- Written Compliance Policies: Annual review required
- Books and Records: Specific retention requirements
- Annual Inspection: Examination readiness
```

### FINRA Considerations
```
If Acting as Broker-Dealer:
- FINRA Series 7, 66 licenses for principals
- Net capital requirements ($250k minimum)
- Customer protection rules
- Market surveillance obligations
- Trade reporting requirements

Our Approach: Partner with existing broker-dealers
- Avoid direct broker-dealer registration
- Revenue sharing agreements
- Regulatory compliance through partners
```

### State Registration Requirements
```
Notice Filings Required in States Where:
- We have clients
- We maintain offices
- We conduct business

Uniform Application Requirements:
- Form U4 for key personnel
- Fingerprinting and background checks
- Bond requirements (varies by state)
- Continuing education requirements
```

## Risk Assessment & Mitigation

### High-Risk Areas
1. **Algorithmic Recommendations**: Potential for unsuitable advice
2. **Performance Claims**: Marketing compliance challenges
3. **Data Security**: Customer information protection
4. **International Expansion**: Multiple regulatory jurisdictions

### Mitigation Strategies
1. **Conservative Algorithm Design**: Emphasis on risk management over returns
2. **Clear Disclaimers**: Prominent risk disclosures throughout app
3. **Robust Security**: Multiple layers of protection and monitoring
4. **Phased International Rollout**: One jurisdiction at a time with local expertise

This compliance framework ensures Refract.trade operates within regulatory boundaries while maintaining its innovative edge and user-focused features.
